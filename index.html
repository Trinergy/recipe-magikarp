Le Fish with a

PH

Description

The simplicity in cooking is what drew me to Chinese cooking, but the complexity in flavor is what made me stay. I learned this recipe from my grandmother and it is always my go-to dish when I want to impress my friends. This is a standard Chinese pan fried fish dish I prefer the texture of the red sea bream for it's tenderness but feel free to use whatever you like.

Ingredients

  • 1 300g Red Sea Bream
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1 ginger root
  • 1/2 bunch green onions
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 cup corn starch
  • 1 cup sake or rice wine
  • 2 cups soy sauce
  • Cooking oil

 

 

 


Instructions

  1. Preparation
    • Scale the fish and take away the innards
    • Wash the fish clean then cut 3 slits on both sides of the fish to ensure the sauce's flavor seeps into the flesh.
    • Generously rub corn starch on both sides of the fish
    • Coarsely chop the vegetables and roots (They are only used for flavoring, not eating)
  2. Cooking
    • Heat the wok or frying pan
    • Add oil and all the coarsely chopped vegetables
    • Let sit for about 5 minutes to properly extract flavor
    • Add fish into the frying pan
    • Immediately pour the liquids into the wok from the side
    • Let it cook for about 7 minutes on the first side
    • Flip the fish onto the other side
    • Add the brown sugar onto the fish and let it cook for another 10 minutes
    • Add a bit of water and put a lid on if you can, it helps cook the flavor into the fish better
    • Voila! Le Fish with a PH
